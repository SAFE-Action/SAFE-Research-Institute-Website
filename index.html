Our Programs

Three Programs

The Foundation conducts its mission through three core programs designed to promote scientific literacy and community health.

Scientific Research

The Evidence Engine

The Foundation convenes a multidisciplinary Scientific Advisory Board—consisting of microbiologists, clinicians, and data scientists—to analyze emerging public health issues and scientific developments. This analysis is compiled into research reports, white papers, and financial impact briefs analyzing the public health costs of emerging scientific issues, published through the Institute's website to make complex scientific information accessible to the public.

Education

Science Awareness & Outreach

The Foundation conducts outreach programs designed to increase scientific literacy, including educational summaries and explanations of public health policy developments, helping the public understand how scientific research informs public health policy and practice. Materials may be delivered through online publications, webinars, public presentations, and other formats.

Technical Assistance

Educational Briefings & Technical Assistance

Upon request, the Foundation provides research-based educational briefings to community organizations or governmental bodies, translating peer-reviewed research into clear educational information.

The Foundation

Why We Exist

The gap between scientific research and public understanding continues to widen. The Science and Freedom for Everyone Foundation was established to make complex scientific information accessible to the public through independent research and evidence-based education.

Our Commitment

As a non-partisan research and educational organization, the Science and Freedom for Everyone Foundation operates under the highest standards of transparency and independence. The Foundation does not support or oppose candidates for public office, does not participate in political campaigns, and does not engage in lobbying activities. All research is conducted independently and published for public benefit.

About the Foundation

During its initial years, the Foundation operates primarily through volunteer support. Activities are conducted virtually using secure digital collaboration tools. All research publications are reviewed and approved by the Scientific Advisory Board prior to release.

The Foundation does not conduct research for the private benefit of any individual, organization, or commercial entity.

The Science and Freedom for Everyone Foundation has applied for recognition as a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Tax-exempt status is pending with the Internal Revenue Service.
