# Pharmacist Immunization Authority Expansion Act

**AN ACT** relating to the practice of pharmacy; expanding the authority of licensed pharmacists to administer vaccines; removing certain referral and prescriptive authority requirements; establishing training and reporting standards; and providing for related matters.

*Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of [STATE]:*

## Section 1. Purpose and Findings

(a) The Legislature finds and declares that:

1. Pharmacists are among the most accessible health care providers in the United States, with approximately ninety percent of Americans living within five miles of a community pharmacy.
2. During the COVID-19 public health emergency, the federal Public Readiness and Emergency Preparedness (PREP) Act and subsequent amendments authorized pharmacists nationwide to administer a broad range of vaccines to individuals aged three and older, demonstrating the safety and efficacy of pharmacist-administered vaccination on a large scale.
3. Data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention indicate that pharmacist-administered vaccinations significantly increased immunization access, particularly in rural and medically underserved communities, during the period of expanded federal authorization.
4. Multiple states have enacted permanent legislation codifying expanded pharmacist vaccination authority following the expiration of federal emergency declarations, resulting in measurable improvements in vaccination coverage rates.
5. Unnecessary referral requirements and restrictive scope-of-practice limitations create barriers to timely immunization, disproportionately affecting populations with limited access to primary care providers.
6. It is in the interest of the state to permanently authorize licensed pharmacists to administer all FDA-approved and FDA-authorized vaccines, subject to appropriate training and reporting requirements, in order to maximize immunization access for all residents.

(b) The purpose of this Act is to permanently expand the scope of practice of licensed pharmacists to include the administration of all FDA-approved and FDA-authorized vaccines to eligible individuals, thereby removing unnecessary barriers to immunization access and codifying the proven public health benefits of pharmacist-administered vaccination.

## Section 2. Definitions

As used in this Act:

1. **"Board"** means the state board of pharmacy or its successor agency.
2. **"Department"** means the state department of health or its successor agency.
3. **"FDA-approved vaccine"** means any vaccine that has received full approval from the United States Food and Drug Administration for use in humans.
4. **"FDA-authorized vaccine"** means any vaccine that has received an Emergency Use Authorization from the United States Food and Drug Administration for use in humans.
5. **"Immunization registry"** means the statewide immunization information system maintained by the department for the purpose of recording and tracking vaccine administration.
6. **"Licensed pharmacist"** means an individual holding an active, unrestricted license to practice pharmacy in this state.
7. **"Pharmacy intern"** means an individual enrolled in an accredited school or college of pharmacy who is practicing under the direct supervision of a licensed pharmacist, as defined by the board.
8. **"Qualified pharmacist"** means a licensed pharmacist who has completed the training requirements set forth in Section 4 of this Act.
9. **"Standing order"** means a written protocol issued by a physician, the department, or the state health officer authorizing the administration of vaccines under specified conditions without requiring an individual patient prescription.

## Section 3. Pharmacist Vaccination Authority

(a) A qualified pharmacist may order, administer, and provide consultation regarding any FDA-approved vaccine or FDA-authorized vaccine to individuals aged three years and older, without requiring:

1. A patient-specific prescription from a physician or other prescriber;
2. A referral from a physician or other health care provider;
3. A collaborative practice agreement with a physician, except as may be otherwise required by the board for specific classes of vaccines administered to individuals aged three through six years; or
4. Prior authorization from a health plan or insurer as a condition of vaccine administration.

(b) A qualified pharmacist may administer vaccines to individuals aged three through six years pursuant to a standing order issued by the department, the state health officer, or a licensed physician, provided that the pharmacist has completed the additional pediatric training requirements established under Section 4(b) of this Act.

(c) A pharmacy intern may administer vaccines under the direct supervision of a qualified pharmacist, provided that the pharmacy intern has completed the training requirements established under Section 4(a) of this Act.

(d) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to:

1. Expand the authority of pharmacists to prescribe non-vaccine medications;
2. Limit the authority of physicians or other licensed health care providers to administer vaccines within their existing scope of practice; or
3. Affect vaccine requirements for school enrollment or other purposes established under existing state law.

## Section 4. Training Requirements

(a) To qualify as a qualified pharmacist under this Act, a licensed pharmacist shall complete and maintain current certification in:

1. An immunization delivery training program accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE) that includes, at minimum:
   - (i) Evidence-based immunization guidelines, including the schedules recommended by the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices, the American Academy of Pediatrics, and the American Academy of Family Physicians;
   - (ii) Vaccine storage, handling, and administration techniques appropriate for all approved routes of administration;
   - (iii) Recognition and treatment of adverse reactions, including anaphylaxis;
   - (iv) Patient screening, contraindication assessment, and informed consent procedures;
   - (v) Requirements for documentation and reporting of administered vaccines.
2. A current certificate in Basic Life Support (BLS) from the American Heart Association, the American Red Cross, or an equivalent nationally recognized provider.

(b) A qualified pharmacist who administers vaccines to individuals aged three through six years shall complete not fewer than two additional hours of continuing education specifically addressing pediatric immunization, including:

1. Age-appropriate dosing and administration techniques for young children;
2. Pediatric-specific contraindications and precautions;
3. Communication strategies for caregivers; and
4. Recognition and management of adverse events specific to pediatric populations.

(c) The board shall establish, by regulation, requirements for ongoing continuing education in immunization delivery as a condition of license renewal, which shall include not fewer than two hours of immunization-related continuing education per biennial renewal period.

## Section 5. Vaccine Administration Protocols

(a) A qualified pharmacist administering vaccines under this Act shall:

1. Screen each patient for contraindications and precautions consistent with current guidelines of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices or, where applicable, the recognized medical professional organizations designated under state law;
2. Provide each patient or the patient's parent or guardian with the current Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) published by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, or the equivalent FDA-required fact sheet for vaccines administered under Emergency Use Authorization;
3. Obtain informed consent from the patient, or from the parent or legal guardian of a minor patient, prior to administration;
4. Administer vaccines in a manner consistent with the manufacturer's labeling and applicable clinical guidelines;
5. Observe the patient for a minimum of fifteen minutes following administration for signs of adverse reaction; and
6. Maintain appropriate emergency supplies, including epinephrine, at the site of administration.

(b) In the event of a significant adverse reaction, the qualified pharmacist shall:

1. Provide immediate appropriate medical intervention;
2. Arrange for transport to an emergency medical facility if clinically indicated;
3. Report the adverse event to the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) in accordance with federal requirements; and
4. Notify the patient's primary care provider, if known, within forty-eight hours.

## Section 6. Documentation and Reporting

(a) A qualified pharmacist who administers a vaccine under this Act shall, within twenty-four hours of administration, report the following information to the immunization registry:

1. The name, date of birth, and address of the patient;
2. The name, manufacturer, lot number, and expiration date of the vaccine administered;
3. The date, route, and anatomic site of administration;
4. The name and license number of the administering pharmacist; and
5. The name and address of the pharmacy at which the vaccine was administered.

(b) A qualified pharmacist shall make reasonable efforts to notify the patient's primary care provider of the vaccination within thirty days of administration, using electronic health information exchange where available.

(c) The pharmacy shall maintain records of all vaccines administered pursuant to this Act for a period of not less than seven years, or as otherwise required by the board.

## Section 7. Insurance Coverage and Reimbursement

(a) No health plan, insurer, or managed care organization operating in this state shall:

1. Require prior authorization for vaccines administered by a qualified pharmacist in accordance with this Act;
2. Impose cost-sharing requirements for pharmacist-administered vaccines that exceed cost-sharing requirements for the same vaccines administered by a physician; or
3. Exclude pharmacist-administered vaccines from coverage that would otherwise apply if the vaccine were administered by a physician.

(b) Reimbursement rates for pharmacist-administered vaccines shall be no less than the rates established for vaccine administration under the state Medicaid program, or the applicable Medicare Part D rate, whichever is greater.

## Section 8. Rulemaking

(a) The board, in consultation with the department and the state medical board, shall promulgate rules necessary to implement this Act within one hundred eighty days of its effective date, including but not limited to:

1. Application and verification procedures for qualified pharmacist designation;
2. Standards for standing orders applicable to vaccination of individuals aged three through six years;
3. Record-keeping and inspection requirements; and
4. Procedures for investigating and addressing complaints related to pharmacist-administered vaccination.

## Section 9. Preemption

(a) This Act shall preempt any local ordinance, regulation, or rule that restricts or limits the authority of a qualified pharmacist to administer vaccines as authorized under this Act.

(b) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to preempt any more permissive local ordinance, regulation, or rule that expands access to pharmacist-administered vaccination beyond the provisions of this Act.

## Section 10. Severability

If any provision of this Act, or its application to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Act are severable.

## Section 11. Effective Date

This Act shall take effect on [DATE], or upon passage and approval, whichever is later.

## Policy Considerations

This model legislation represents one approach to permanently expanding pharmacist vaccination authority. Legislators considering this template should evaluate:

- Scope of practice boundaries and physician oversight requirements, particularly for vaccination of young children aged three through six where pediatric clinical judgment may be especially important
- Liability and malpractice insurance implications for pharmacists administering a broader range of vaccines, and whether existing pharmacy liability frameworks adequately address expanded duties
- The impact on care coordination when vaccinations occur outside the primary care setting, including the reliability of notification mechanisms to patients' primary care providers
- Fiscal impact on state budgets and healthcare systems
- Compatibility with existing state statutes and regulatory frameworks

The SAFE Research Institute presents this template as an educational resource and does not advocate for or against any specific legislative outcome.
