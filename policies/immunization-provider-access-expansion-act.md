# Immunization Provider Access Expansion Act

**AN ACT** relating to public health; expanding the categories of health care professionals authorized to administer vaccines; establishing supervision, training, and certification requirements; and providing for related matters.

*Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of [STATE]:*

## Section 1. Purpose

(a) The Legislature finds and declares that:

1. Timely access to immunization services is essential to protecting individual and community health, yet significant access gaps persist in rural, frontier, and medically underserved communities across the state.
2. Existing restrictions on which health care professionals may administer vaccines limit the capacity of health care delivery systems to meet patient demand, particularly during periods of heightened need such as seasonal influenza campaigns and public health emergencies.
3. Multiple categories of trained health care professionals possess the clinical competency to safely administer vaccines under appropriate supervision, yet are not currently authorized to do so under state law.
4. Expanding the categories of authorized immunization providers, with proper safeguards, will increase vaccination access, reduce wait times, and improve immunization coverage rates without compromising patient safety.

(b) The purpose of this Act is to authorize additional categories of health care professionals to administer vaccines under defined supervision and training requirements, thereby expanding immunization access throughout the state.

## Section 2. Definitions

As used in this Act:

1. **"Authorized immunization provider"** means any individual authorized under this Act or other provisions of state law to administer vaccines.
2. **"Certified medical assistant"** means an individual who holds a current certification from a nationally recognized medical assistant credentialing organization and is employed by or under contract with a licensed health care facility or practitioner.
3. **"Direct supervision"** means that a supervising practitioner is present in the same facility and immediately available to provide assistance and direction during the administration of a vaccine.
4. **"Nursing student"** means an individual enrolled in an accredited registered nursing or practical nursing program who has completed coursework in pharmacology and injection technique.
5. **"Registered pharmacy technician"** means an individual registered as a pharmacy technician with the state board of pharmacy who is in good standing.
6. **"Supervising practitioner"** means a physician, advanced practice registered nurse, physician assistant, or pharmacist licensed in this state.

## Section 3. Authorized Immunization Providers

(a) In addition to those health care professionals currently authorized to administer vaccines under state law, the following individuals may administer vaccines in accordance with this Act:

1. Registered pharmacy technicians, under the direct supervision of a licensed pharmacist;
2. Certified medical assistants, under the direct supervision of a licensed physician, advanced practice registered nurse, or physician assistant;
3. Nursing students who have completed the training requirements of Section 5 of this Act, under the direct supervision of a licensed registered nurse, advanced practice registered nurse, or physician;
4. Emergency medical technicians certified at the basic level or above, under the direct supervision of a physician or pursuant to standing orders issued by a physician medical director.

(b) An authorized immunization provider under this section may administer any vaccine included on the immunization schedule published by the state department of health, subject to the supervision and training requirements of this Act.

## Section 4. Supervision Requirements

(a) No individual authorized solely under this Act shall administer a vaccine unless a supervising practitioner is present in the same facility and immediately available.

(b) A supervising practitioner may supervise no more than four authorized immunization providers simultaneously.

(c) The supervising practitioner shall review and co-sign all immunization records completed by an authorized immunization provider under this Act within twenty-four hours of vaccine administration.

(d) The supervising practitioner shall be responsible for ensuring that all pre-screening assessments, including review of contraindications and patient history, are completed prior to vaccine administration.

## Section 5. Training and Certification

(a) Prior to administering vaccines under this Act, each authorized immunization provider shall complete a training program that includes, at minimum:

1. Not fewer than four hours of didactic instruction covering vaccine storage and handling, immunization schedules, contraindications, proper injection technique, and infection control procedures;
2. A hands-on clinical practicum including the administration of not fewer than five injections under direct observation by a supervising practitioner;
3. Current certification in cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) and basic first aid;
4. Training in the recognition and emergency management of anaphylaxis and other adverse reactions, including the use of epinephrine auto-injectors.

(b) Training programs under this section shall be approved by the state department of health and may be offered by accredited educational institutions, health care facilities, professional associations, or the department itself.

(c) Each authorized immunization provider shall complete not fewer than two hours of continuing education in immunization practice annually to maintain authorization under this Act.

## Section 6. Liability Protections

(a) An authorized immunization provider who administers a vaccine in compliance with this Act and within the scope of their authorization shall be entitled to the same liability protections available under federal and state law to other health care professionals who administer vaccines.

(b) A supervising practitioner shall not incur additional liability solely by reason of supervising an authorized immunization provider under this Act, provided that the supervising practitioner has fulfilled the supervision and oversight obligations set forth in Section 4.

(c) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to limit or alter the protections afforded under the National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act of 1986 or the Public Readiness and Emergency Preparedness Act.

## Section 7. Reporting Requirements

(a) Each authorized immunization provider shall report all vaccines administered to the state immunization information system within twenty-four hours of administration, in accordance with existing state reporting requirements.

(b) The state department of health shall publish an annual report summarizing the number of vaccines administered by authorized immunization providers under this Act, including data disaggregated by provider category, geographic region, and patient demographics.

(c) Any adverse event following immunization administered by an authorized immunization provider under this Act shall be reported in accordance with existing state and federal adverse event reporting requirements, including submission to the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS).

## Section 8. Effective Date

This Act shall take effect one hundred eighty days after enactment, provided that the state department of health shall promulgate regulations necessary for implementation within one hundred twenty days of enactment.

## Policy Considerations

This model legislation represents one approach to expanding the immunization workforce through broadened scope of practice. Legislators considering this template should evaluate:

- Scope of practice boundaries and the positions of state medical boards, nursing boards, and pharmacy boards regarding delegation of vaccine administration to additional provider categories
- Whether the specified supervision ratios and training requirements are sufficient to maintain patient safety, particularly for pediatric populations and individuals with complex medical histories
- Liability and malpractice insurance implications for newly authorized provider categories and their supervising practitioners
- Fiscal impact on state budgets and healthcare systems
- Compatibility with existing state statutes and regulatory frameworks

The SAFE Research Institute presents this template as an educational resource and does not advocate for or against any specific legislative outcome.
