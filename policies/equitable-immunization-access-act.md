# Equitable Immunization Access Act

**AN ACT** relating to public health; requiring the identification and remediation of immunization deserts; establishing mobile vaccination programs, community health worker initiatives, and multilingual outreach; creating the Immunization Equity Fund; and providing for related matters.

*Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of [STATE]:*

## Section 1. Purpose and Findings

(a) The Legislature finds and declares that:

1. Immunization is a cornerstone of public health, yet significant disparities in vaccination rates persist across the state based on race, ethnicity, household income, geographic location, and primary language.
2. Data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and state immunization registries demonstrate that communities of color, rural populations, and low-income households consistently experience lower vaccination rates for routine childhood, adolescent, and adult immunizations compared to state and national averages.
3. Geographic analysis reveals that many areas of the state qualify as immunization deserts, defined as communities where residents face significant barriers to accessing vaccination services due to distance, provider scarcity, transportation limitations, or inadequate culturally and linguistically appropriate services.
4. The COVID-19 pandemic further exposed and exacerbated existing immunization disparities, with Black, Latino, Indigenous, and rural communities experiencing disproportionately lower vaccination rates and higher rates of vaccine-preventable morbidity and mortality.
5. Evidence-based interventions, including mobile vaccination units, community health worker programs, and multilingual outreach campaigns, have been demonstrated to improve vaccination rates in underserved populations when implemented with sustained funding and community engagement.
6. Multiple states and localities have successfully piloted or permanently funded programs to address immunization deserts, providing replicable models for statewide implementation.
7. It is in the interest of the state to systematically identify and address immunization deserts and to eliminate disparities in immunization access to protect the health of all residents.

(b) The purpose of this Act is to establish a comprehensive framework for identifying immunization deserts, deploying targeted interventions to improve immunization access in underserved communities, and eliminating disparities in vaccination rates based on race, ethnicity, income, geography, and language.

## Section 2. Definitions

As used in this Act:

1. **"Community health worker"** means a frontline public health worker who is a trusted member of, or has an unusually close understanding of, the community served, and who serves as a liaison between health services and the community to facilitate access to services, improve the quality and cultural competence of service delivery, and promote health equity.
2. **"Department"** means the state department of health or its successor agency.
3. **"Director"** means the director of the department or the director's designee.
4. **"Federally qualified health center"** means an entity that has been designated as a federally qualified health center under Section 330 of the Public Health Service Act (42 U.S.C. 254b).
5. **"Immunization desert"** means a geographic area designated by the department under Section 3 of this Act in which residents face significant barriers to accessing immunization services.
6. **"Immunization Equity Fund"** means the fund established under Section 8 of this Act.
7. **"Immunization registry"** means the statewide immunization information system maintained by the department for the purpose of recording and tracking vaccine administration.
8. **"Limited English proficiency"** means the inability to adequately understand or communicate effectively in English, as determined by standards established by the department.
9. **"Local health department"** means a county, municipal, or regional health department or district operating within the state.
10. **"Mobile vaccination unit"** means a vehicle, temporary facility, or portable clinic equipped and staffed to administer vaccines at locations outside of traditional health care settings.
11. **"Underserved population"** means a population group that experiences disparities in immunization coverage rates due to barriers including but not limited to geographic isolation, poverty, lack of health insurance, racial or ethnic discrimination, disability, homelessness, or limited English proficiency.

## Section 3. Identification of Immunization Deserts

(a) The department shall, within one hundred eighty days of the effective date of this Act and annually thereafter, conduct a comprehensive assessment to identify immunization deserts within the state. The assessment shall consider:

1. Geographic distribution of immunization providers, including physicians, pharmacies, federally qualified health centers, local health departments, and other entities authorized to administer vaccines;
2. Travel time and distance from population centers to the nearest immunization provider, with particular attention to public transportation availability;
3. Immunization coverage rates disaggregated by census tract, zip code, county, race, ethnicity, age group, and insurance status, utilizing data from the immunization registry, the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System, and other available data sources;
4. Socioeconomic indicators including poverty rates, rates of uninsurance and underinsurance, and rates of limited English proficiency;
5. Availability of culturally and linguistically appropriate immunization services; and
6. Input from community organizations, local health departments, and residents of potentially affected communities.

(b) The department shall designate as an immunization desert any geographic area that meets one or more of the following criteria:

1. The area has an immunization coverage rate for any routinely recommended vaccine that is more than ten percentage points below the statewide average for the relevant age group;
2. Residents of the area must travel more than thirty minutes by available transportation to reach the nearest immunization provider;
3. The ratio of immunization providers to population within the area falls below a threshold established by the department by regulation; or
4. The area demonstrates a pattern of persistent immunization disparities affecting underserved populations, as determined by the department based on the assessment conducted under subsection (a).

(c) The department shall publish a map of designated immunization deserts on its website, updated annually, and shall submit a report of its findings and designations to the Governor and the Legislature.

## Section 4. Mobile Vaccination Unit Program

(a) The department shall establish and administer a Mobile Vaccination Unit Program to deliver immunization services directly to communities within designated immunization deserts.

(b) The program shall:

1. Deploy not fewer than one mobile vaccination unit per designated immunization desert, or per region containing multiple adjacent immunization deserts, within twelve months of the designation;
2. Operate mobile vaccination units on a regular, predictable schedule, with each designated immunization desert receiving service not less than twice per month;
3. Offer all routinely recommended vaccines for children, adolescents, and adults, as established by the state immunization schedule;
4. Provide immunization services at no cost to uninsured and underinsured individuals, utilizing Vaccines for Children program stock, state-purchased vaccine, and other available resources;
5. Staff each mobile vaccination unit with at least one licensed health care provider authorized to administer vaccines and at least one community health worker or patient navigator;
6. Coordinate with local health departments, schools, houses of worship, community organizations, employers, and other community-based institutions to identify optimal deployment locations and schedules; and
7. Maintain supplies and equipment necessary for proper vaccine storage, administration, and management of adverse events.

(c) The department may contract with local health departments, federally qualified health centers, hospital systems, pharmacy organizations, or other qualified entities to operate mobile vaccination units under this section.

(d) The department shall maintain records of all vaccines administered through the Mobile Vaccination Unit Program and shall report program data to the immunization registry in accordance with existing state law.

## Section 5. Community Health Worker Immunization Program

(a) The department shall establish a Community Health Worker Immunization Program to employ and deploy community health workers in designated immunization deserts for the purpose of improving immunization awareness, education, and uptake.

(b) Community health workers employed or contracted under this program shall:

1. Conduct door-to-door and community-based outreach to identify unvaccinated and undervaccinated individuals;
2. Provide culturally appropriate education regarding the safety, efficacy, and benefits of recommended immunizations;
3. Assist individuals in scheduling vaccination appointments, obtaining transportation to immunization providers, and navigating insurance coverage and financial assistance programs;
4. Address vaccine hesitancy through evidence-based motivational interviewing and trusted messenger strategies;
5. Provide follow-up to ensure completion of multi-dose vaccine series;
6. Collect and report data on barriers to immunization identified through community engagement; and
7. Serve as liaisons between underserved communities and the formal health care system.

(c) The department shall establish training requirements for community health workers participating in this program, which shall include, at minimum:

1. Overview of the recommended immunization schedules and the diseases they prevent;
2. Evidence-based techniques for addressing vaccine hesitancy and misinformation;
3. Cultural competency and health literacy principles;
4. Privacy and confidentiality requirements under state and federal law; and
5. Documentation and reporting requirements.

(d) The department shall prioritize the recruitment and hiring of community health workers who are members of the underserved populations and communities they will serve, including individuals who are bilingual or multilingual.

## Section 6. Multilingual Outreach and Communication

(a) The department shall develop and implement a comprehensive multilingual immunization outreach and communication strategy that includes:

1. Translation of all immunization-related educational materials, including vaccine information statements, appointment scheduling tools, consent forms, and public awareness campaigns, into the ten most commonly spoken non-English languages in the state, as determined by the most recent data from the American Community Survey;
2. Provision of interpreter services, including telephonic and in-person interpretation, at all state-operated and state-funded immunization sites for individuals with limited English proficiency;
3. Development of culturally adapted immunization awareness campaigns that are designed in partnership with community organizations representing underserved populations and that utilize trusted community channels including ethnic media, community radio, social media platforms, and faith-based organizations;
4. Establishment of a multilingual immunization information hotline, staffed during regular business hours, to provide vaccine information, assist with appointment scheduling, and connect callers with community health workers; and
5. Collaboration with school districts, Head Start programs, and childcare providers serving communities with high rates of limited English proficiency to distribute immunization information and facilitate vaccination.

(b) The department shall ensure that all public-facing digital platforms and web-based tools related to immunization, including the immunization registry portal and online appointment scheduling systems, are accessible in the languages identified under subsection (a)(1) and comply with applicable accessibility standards.

## Section 7. Data Collection and Accountability

(a) The department shall collect, analyze, and publish data annually on immunization coverage rates disaggregated by:

1. Race and ethnicity;
2. Age group;
3. Geographic area, including by county and census tract;
4. Insurance status;
5. Primary language; and
6. Household income, to the extent such data is available.

(b) The department shall establish measurable goals for eliminating immunization disparities in designated immunization deserts, including:

1. Achieving immunization coverage rates within five percentage points of the statewide average for all routinely recommended vaccines in each designated immunization desert within three years of designation;
2. Reducing racial and ethnic disparities in immunization coverage by fifty percent within five years of the effective date of this Act; and
3. Ensuring that no geographic area of the state qualifies as an immunization desert within seven years of the effective date of this Act.

(c) The department shall submit a biennial report to the Governor and the Legislature on progress toward the goals established under subsection (b), including an assessment of the effectiveness of the programs established under this Act and recommendations for additional legislative or administrative action.

(d) The department shall convene an Immunization Equity Advisory Council, composed of representatives of underserved populations, community health workers, local health department officials, immunization providers, and public health researchers, to advise the department on the implementation and evaluation of programs under this Act.

## Section 8. Immunization Equity Fund

(a) There is hereby created in the state treasury a special fund to be known as the Immunization Equity Fund.

(b) The fund shall consist of:

1. Appropriations made by the Legislature for the purposes of this Act;
2. Federal grants and funds allocated to the state for immunization equity, access, or outreach purposes;
3. Donations, gifts, and bequests made to the fund; and
4. Any other funds designated by law for deposit into the fund.

(c) Moneys in the fund shall be used exclusively for:

1. Operation of the Mobile Vaccination Unit Program established under Section 4;
2. Operation of the Community Health Worker Immunization Program established under Section 5;
3. Multilingual outreach and communication activities under Section 6;
4. Data collection, analysis, and reporting under Section 7;
5. Vaccine purchase for uninsured and underinsured individuals in designated immunization deserts; and
6. Administrative costs directly associated with the programs established under this Act, not to exceed ten percent of total annual fund expenditures.

(d) The department shall publish an annual accounting of all expenditures from the fund on its website.

## Section 9. Non-Discrimination

(a) No program or service established under this Act shall discriminate against any individual on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity, immigration status, or primary language.

(b) Immunization services provided under this Act shall be available to all eligible individuals regardless of insurance status, ability to pay, or documentation of citizenship or immigration status.

## Section 10. Coordination with Existing Programs

(a) The department shall coordinate the implementation of this Act with existing federal, state, and local immunization programs, including but not limited to:

1. The Vaccines for Children (VFC) program;
2. The Section 317 Immunization Program;
3. Programs administered by federally qualified health centers;
4. School-based health programs; and
5. Maternal and child health programs.

(b) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to duplicate, supplant, or reduce funding for existing immunization programs.

## Section 11. Severability

If any provision of this Act, or its application to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Act are severable.

## Section 12. Effective Date

This Act shall take effect on [DATE], or upon passage and approval, whichever is later.

## Policy Considerations

This model legislation represents one approach to addressing geographic and demographic disparities in immunization access. Legislators considering this template should evaluate:

- Whether designated immunization desert criteria appropriately reflect local conditions, given wide variation in rural versus urban healthcare infrastructure across states
- The fiscal impact of establishing and sustaining mobile vaccination units, community health worker programs, and multilingual outreach at scale, including ongoing operational costs beyond initial appropriations
- Potential overlap or conflict with existing federal programs such as Vaccines for Children (VFC) and Section 317, and whether state funds would supplement or inadvertently duplicate federal resources
- Fiscal impact on state budgets and healthcare systems
- Compatibility with existing state statutes and regulatory frameworks

The SAFE Research Institute presents this template as an educational resource and does not advocate for or against any specific legislative outcome.
