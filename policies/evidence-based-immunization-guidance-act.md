# Evidence-Based Immunization Guidance Act

**AN ACT** relating to public health; authorizing the state department of health to establish immunization recommendations based on guidance from recognized medical professional organizations; and providing for related matters.

*Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of [STATE]:*

## Section 1. Purpose and Findings

(a) The Legislature finds and declares that:

1. Immunization is among the most effective public health interventions in history, preventing millions of cases of disease, disability, and death each year.
2. The health and welfare of the residents of this state depend upon timely access to immunizations guided by the best available scientific and clinical evidence.
3. Recognized medical professional organizations, through rigorous peer-reviewed processes, develop and maintain immunization recommendations grounded in current epidemiological data, clinical trial evidence, and established principles of preventive medicine.
4. It is in the interest of the state to ensure that its immunization recommendations remain aligned with the prevailing scientific consensus, even in the event of disruption, delay, or alteration of federal advisory processes.

(b) The purpose of this Act is to authorize the state department of health to promulgate immunization schedules and recommendations informed by the guidance of recognized medical professional organizations, thereby safeguarding continuity of evidence-based immunization practice within the state.

## Section 2. Definitions

As used in this Act:

1. **"Department"** means the state department of health or its successor agency.
2. **"Director"** means the director of the department or the director's designee.
3. **"Immunization recommendation"** means a clinical guideline specifying the vaccines, dosing schedules, age-appropriate timing, and target populations for immunization within the state.
4. **"Recognized medical professional organization"** means an organization designated under Section 4 of this Act.
5. **"State Immunization Advisory Committee"** means the committee established under Section 3 of this Act.

## Section 3. State Immunization Advisory Authority

(a) There is hereby established within the department a State Immunization Advisory Committee, composed of not fewer than nine members appointed by the director, including:

1. At least three physicians licensed in the state with expertise in infectious disease, pediatrics, or preventive medicine;
2. At least one representative from the state's public health laboratory system;
3. At least one immunologist or vaccinologist affiliated with an accredited research institution;
4. At least one representative of a federally qualified health center operating within the state;
5. At least two public members, one of whom shall have expertise in health equity.

(b) The committee shall convene no fewer than four times per calendar year and shall advise the director on the adoption, modification, or withdrawal of immunization recommendations.

(c) The director, upon consultation with the committee, shall have the authority to issue, amend, or rescind immunization recommendations applicable within the state.

## Section 4. Recognized Medical Professional Organizations

(a) For purposes of this Act, the following organizations are designated as recognized medical professional organizations:

1. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP);
2. The American Academy of Family Physicians (AAFP);
3. The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG);
4. The American College of Physicians (ACP);
5. The American Medical Association (AMA).

(b) The director may, by regulation and upon recommendation of the committee, designate additional organizations as recognized medical professional organizations provided that such organizations maintain transparent, peer-reviewed processes for developing clinical immunization guidance.

## Section 5. Baseline Recommendations

(a) The department shall maintain a baseline immunization schedule that, at minimum, incorporates the most recent immunization recommendations published by any two or more of the recognized medical professional organizations listed in Section 4.

(b) In the event that federal immunization recommendations are suspended, materially delayed, or diverge from the prevailing consensus of the recognized medical professional organizations, the department shall issue interim guidance consistent with the consensus of such organizations.

(c) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to prohibit the department from adopting recommendations that exceed federal recommendations where supported by the scientific evidence reviewed by the committee.

## Section 6. Periodic Review

(a) The committee shall conduct a comprehensive review of the state immunization schedule not less than once per calendar year, taking into account:

1. Updated guidance from the recognized medical professional organizations;
2. Current epidemiological surveillance data from within the state and nationally;
3. Emerging evidence from peer-reviewed scientific literature;
4. Immunization coverage rates and disparities among populations within the state.

(b) Following each review, the committee shall publish a written report summarizing its findings and any recommended changes to the state immunization schedule. Such report shall be made publicly available on the department's website.

## Section 7. Severability

If any provision of this Act, or its application to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Act are severable.

## Policy Considerations

This model legislation represents one approach to establishing state-level immunization scheduling authority. Legislators considering this template should evaluate:

- The potential for divergence between state and federal immunization recommendations, and the resulting complexity for healthcare providers, insurers, and patients navigating different schedules
- Whether the designated medical professional organizations adequately represent the range of clinical perspectives, and the process for adding or removing organizations from the list
- Administrative costs and expertise required to staff and sustain a State Immunization Advisory Committee with the technical capacity to independently evaluate vaccine evidence
- Fiscal impact on state budgets and healthcare systems
- Compatibility with existing state statutes and regulatory frameworks

The SAFE Research Institute presents this template as an educational resource and does not advocate for or against any specific legislative outcome.
