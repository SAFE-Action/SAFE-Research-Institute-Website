# Vaccine Coverage Guarantee Act

**AN ACT** relating to health insurance; requiring coverage of state-recommended immunizations without cost-sharing; establishing provider reimbursement standards; and providing for enforcement and related matters.

*Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of [STATE]:*

## Section 1. Purpose

The Legislature finds that financial barriers to immunization, including copayments, deductibles, and prior authorization requirements, reduce vaccination rates and disproportionately affect low-income families, older adults, and medically underserved populations. This Act ensures that all residents covered by state-regulated health insurance plans can receive recommended immunizations without encountering cost barriers at the point of care.

## Section 2. Definitions

As used in this Act:

1. **"Cost-sharing"** means any copayment, coinsurance, deductible, or other out-of-pocket expense imposed on an enrollee at the time an immunization service is rendered.
2. **"Covered immunization"** means any vaccine or combination of vaccines included on the immunization schedule published by the state department of health, including those administered for routine, catch-up, or risk-based indications.
3. **"Health benefit plan"** means any individual or group health insurance policy, health care service plan contract, or managed care plan issued, delivered, or renewed in this state, including plans offered through the state health benefit exchange. This term does not include excepted benefit plans, short-term limited duration insurance, or plans administered by a federal agency.
4. **"Issuer"** means any health insurance company, health care service plan, or managed care organization authorized to transact business in this state.
5. **"Administering provider"** means any health care professional or facility authorized under state law to administer vaccines, including physicians, advanced practice providers, pharmacists, and pharmacies.

## Section 3. Coverage Requirements

(a) Every health benefit plan issued, delivered, amended, or renewed in this state on or after the effective date of this Act shall provide coverage for all covered immunizations.

(b) Coverage shall include the vaccine product, its administration, and any related office visit or clinical evaluation necessary for the immunization to be provided, when the immunization is the primary purpose of the visit.

(c) Coverage shall extend to immunizations administered by any administering provider acting within their scope of practice, regardless of whether the provider is within the plan's contracted network.

## Section 4. Prohibited Cost-Sharing

(a) No issuer shall impose any cost-sharing on an enrollee for a covered immunization, regardless of whether the immunization is provided by an in-network or out-of-network administering provider.

(b) No issuer shall require prior authorization, step therapy, or any other utilization management technique as a condition of coverage for a covered immunization.

(c) No issuer shall apply any annual or lifetime limit specific to immunization benefits under this Act.

(d) An issuer shall not classify immunization services under a separate benefit tier or category that results in less favorable coverage than other preventive services.

## Section 5. Provider Reimbursement Standards

(a) Issuers shall reimburse administering providers for covered immunizations at a rate not less than the applicable rate published under the Medicare Part B physician fee schedule, or the rate negotiated under the provider's contract with the issuer, whichever is greater.

(b) For out-of-network administering providers, issuers shall reimburse at not less than one hundred percent of the Medicare Part B rate for the vaccine product and its administration.

(c) Reimbursement shall be rendered within thirty calendar days of submission of a clean claim.

## Section 6. Pharmacy Administration Coverage

(a) Issuers shall provide coverage for immunizations administered at licensed pharmacies on the same terms and conditions as immunizations administered in a physician's office.

(b) No issuer shall require an enrollee to obtain a prescription as a condition of coverage for a vaccine that may be lawfully administered by a pharmacist under state pharmacy practice laws.

(c) Pharmacies shall be reimbursed for both the vaccine product and a reasonable administration fee, which shall not be less than the administration fee established under the Medicare Part D vaccine program.

## Section 7. Implementation Timeline

(a) This Act shall apply to all health benefit plans issued or renewed on or after January 1 of the calendar year following enactment.

(b) Issuers shall update their formularies, provider communications, and claims processing systems to comply with this Act within one hundred eighty days of enactment.

(c) The department of insurance shall issue guidance to issuers regarding compliance with this Act within ninety days of enactment.

## Section 8. Enforcement

(a) The department of insurance shall enforce this Act and may examine issuer compliance as part of routine market conduct examinations.

(b) Any issuer found in violation of this Act shall be subject to corrective action, including:

1. An order to provide retroactive reimbursement to affected enrollees for any improperly assessed cost-sharing;
2. A civil penalty of not more than one thousand dollars per violation per enrollee;
3. Suspension or revocation of the issuer's certificate of authority for repeated or willful violations.

(c) An enrollee aggrieved by a violation of this Act may file a complaint with the department of insurance and shall have a private right of action to recover actual damages, reasonable attorney fees, and costs.

## Policy Considerations

This model legislation represents one approach to eliminating cost-sharing barriers for immunization services. Legislators considering this template should evaluate:

- The impact on insurance premiums and overall healthcare costs, as mandated zero cost-sharing benefits are ultimately distributed across the insured risk pool
- Whether requiring coverage of out-of-network immunization providers at Medicare rates creates adequate reimbursement or may lead to provider access issues in some markets
- The interaction with federal ACA preventive services mandates and ERISA preemption, which limits the applicability of state insurance mandates to self-funded employer plans
- Fiscal impact on state budgets and healthcare systems
- Compatibility with existing state statutes and regulatory frameworks

The SAFE Research Institute presents this template as an educational resource and does not advocate for or against any specific legislative outcome.
